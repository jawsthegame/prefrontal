Most productivity tools assume you'll remember to use them.

Prefrontal doesn't. It watches your context — location, calendar, time of day, recent activity — decides on its own when to speak up, and nudges through the channel you'll actually notice. Then it learns from whether the nudge worked. It gets better the longer you use it.

⚙ Under the hood — for the curious

A system of small, single-purpose agents over a local SQLite behavioral memory — the core everything learns into. Capture → triage → memory → coaching → delivery. A coaching tick engine asks each module "anything due?" and picks the channel; a FastAPI surface takes one-tap input from iOS Shortcuts; n8n orchestrates; Ollama runs inference on-device (optional Claude per task); reminders escalate over Pushover / ntfy / TTS with one-tap action buttons; everything's reachable over Tailscale, multi-user. ADHD isn't one thing — so Prefrontal isn't one nudge.

Executive function fails in distinct ways: you lose the feel of time, you can't start, you can't stop, you switch on impulse. Each module targets one of those failure modes with its own trigger and its own intervention — built on the same shared behavioral memory, so they learn together.

Why split it up? A single "reminder" can't tell paralysis from hyperfocus. Naming the dysfunction lets each response be the right one.

Time perception & estimation
⏰Time Blindness
time_blindness
Difficulty sensing elapsed time and estimating duration — chronic underestimation of how long tasks and travel really take, and departures that slip late.
How it shows up

"Five more minutes" becomes forty; you leave for the 2:45 appointment at 2:40 because it feels close.

What Prefrontal does

Corrects your estimates from your real history, pads departures with a learned buffer, and calls out elapsed time mid-task soon.

Task initiation
🧊Task Paralysis
task_paralysis
Difficulty initiating tasks despite intent — the work stalls before it ever begins, especially when it's large or ambiguous.
How it shows up

You know exactly what to do and still can't start; the draft sits open, untouched, for an hour.

What Prefrontal does

Shrinks it to a 5-minute first step, auto-decomposes the big/vague thing into moves, and offers a body-double check-in.

Focus regulation
🎯Hyperfocus
hyperfocus
Intense sustained focus — a strength when aimed right, a liability when misdirected or when it overruns basic needs and commitments.
How it shows up

Three hours into a side rabbit-hole; the real deadline and lunch both slid by unnoticed.

What Prefrontal does

Protects the window when focus is on-target, checks alignment when it may be off, enforces biological breaks, and auto-closes abandoned sessions.

Response inhibition
⚡Impulsivity
impulsivity
Acting before reflecting — abrupt context switches and snap decisions that pull attention off the intended task.
How it shows up

A ping pulls you into a new tab, then a chore, then something else — the original task never comes back.

What Prefrontal does

Inserts a reflective pause before a snap switch, captures the distraction to defer it, and surfaces your switch rate soon.

Prospective memory, out in the world
📍Location-Aware Task Anchor
location_anchor
Leaving with a short, stated intention and losing track of time in the field — errands that quietly stretch well past when you meant to be back.
How it shows up

"Just running to the store" turns into a two-hour loop of unplanned stops.

What Prefrontal does

Escalating field nudges — soft at your stated time, firm when you're over, a voice call if you go far past — all gated by actually being on-site.
