Modules answer how your ADHD shows up. A Pack answers what life you're managing.

Parenting with ADHD is an executive-function stress test — departures, hand-offs, the invisible inventory of sizes, appointments and who's on pickup. The Parent Pack doesn't add another app; it composes the primitives Prefrontal already runs — departure, panic, task-breakdown, coaching — into the shape of a parent's day, and adds one shared sheet both co-parents keep current. Turn it on; it wires the rest.

→ What the Pack pre-wires (roadmap)

One-tap setup. Switches on the challenge modules a parent needs and seeds the vocabulary — a child appointment kind, kid-tagged todos, school-run windows.
Situation tools. Thin compositions of the primitives — school-run departure, pack-the-bag checklist, "who's covering pickup?", sick-day replan — no new app, just wiring.
Shared co-parent sheet. Both parents read and write one household sheet in plain English — sizes, routines, allergies, providers, behavior plans, appointments.
Delta digest. Every entry stamped who-set-it-when; the coaching layer pushes the changes that matter to the parent who didn't make them.
Tailored family view. One glanceable sheet — kids' facts · agreements · upcoming appts · recently changed — reusing the profile render, not a new UI.
Shareable. "Install the Parent pack" — the same opt-in, modular ethos as challenge modules, on the orthogonal life-context axis.
The shared sheet →
Everything you need to step in and help — any time, no asking required. Shoe size, the EpiPen, the sticker plan, who's the dentist — each stamped with who updated it and when, so "who's carrying the mental load" is finally legible, and the deltas get pushed to the other parent instead of living in one head.

⚙ How a Pack works — for the curious

A Pack is a composition layer, not a Module subclass — modules sit on the executive-function axis, "parent" on the orthogonal life-context axis. It's mostly declarative plus a small tool registry: it (1) enables a subset of challenge modules, (2) seeds domain vocabulary — commitment kinds, todo categories, default windows, (3) registers situation tools that compose shipped primitives — todos, commitments, departure, panic, decompose_task, the NL-assistant whitelist — and (4) tailors the family view. Nudges route through the same coaching tick engine; the co-parent sheet adds a household scope alongside per-user multi-tenancy — two users, one set of rows, every write attributed.
